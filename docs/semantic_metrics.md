# Introduction

MetricMind is an AI-powered Business Intelligence (BI) system that answers business questions using a governed Semantic Layer instead of directly querying raw database tables.

This document defines the core business metrics that MetricMind should support. These standardized metric definitions will serve as the foundation for the Semantic Layer (Cube.dev/dbt), ensuring that business users, dashboards, and AI-generated responses all use the same business logic.

Having a single source of truth prevents inconsistent calculations, improves governance, and enables trustworthy AI-driven analytics.

---

# 1. Objective

Before building the Semantic Layer or AI orchestration logic, the project requires a clear and standardized definition of the business metrics that MetricMind will support.

This document aims to:

- Define common business metrics used in Business Intelligence.
- Standardize formulas for each metric.
- Identify the dimensions used to analyze those metrics.
- Provide a reference for future Semantic Layer implementation using Cube.dev or dbt.
- Ensure consistent AI-generated insights across the organization.

This directly addresses the project's core problem:

> Large Language Models (LLMs) should not generate business metrics by writing ad-hoc SQL. Instead, they should retrieve pre-defined semantic metrics so that every user receives accurate and consistent results.

---

# 2. Why Semantic Metrics are Important

Without a Semantic Layer, different users or AI models may calculate the same business metric differently, leading to inconsistent reports and loss of trust.

| Without Semantic Metrics | With Semantic Metrics |
|--------------------------|-----------------------|
| AI generates custom SQL for every question | AI retrieves pre-defined semantic metrics |
| Different teams calculate metrics differently | All teams use the same standardized formula |
| Business logic exists only in documentation or people's knowledge | Business logic is stored as reusable code |
| Difficult to audit reports | Every metric is transparent and traceable |
| High risk of incorrect business decisions | Reliable and governed analytics |

A semantic metric is more than a database column—it represents a well-defined business concept with a standardized formula and supported dimensions.

# 3. Common Business Intelligence Metrics

Business Intelligence dashboards typically organize metrics into several categories.

## 3.1 Revenue Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Description |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| Gross Revenue | Σ(Unit Price × Quantity) | Total sales before returns and discounts |
| Net Revenue | Gross Revenue − Returns − Discounts | Actual business revenue after deductions |
| Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) | Σ(Subscription Revenue) | Monthly recurring subscription income |
| Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) | MRR × 12 | Annual subscription revenue |

---

## 3.2 Profitability Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Description |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| Gross Profit | Revenue − Cost of Goods Sold | Profit after production costs |
| Gross Margin | (Gross Profit / Revenue) × 100 | Profitability percentage |
| Operating Margin | Operating Income / Revenue | Business efficiency after operating expenses |
| Contribution Margin | (Revenue − Variable Costs) / Revenue | Profit generated after variable costs |

---

## 3.3 Customer Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Description |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| Customer Churn Rate | Customers Lost / Customers at Start | Percentage of customers lost |
| Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) | Average Revenue × Customer Lifespan | Total value generated by a customer |
| Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) | Marketing Cost / New Customers | Cost of acquiring one customer |
| Active Customers | Count(Distinct Customer ID) | Number of active customers |

---

## 3.4 Growth Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Description |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| Month-over-Month Growth | (Current − Previous) / Previous | Monthly growth percentage |
| Quarter-over-Quarter Growth | (Current Quarter − Previous Quarter) / Previous Quarter | Quarterly growth |
| Year-over-Year Growth | (Current Year − Previous Year) / Previous Year | Annual growth |
| Revenue Growth | (Current Revenue − Previous Revenue) / Previous Revenue | Revenue increase over time |

---

## 3.5 Operational Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Description |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| Total Orders | Count(Order ID) | Total completed orders |
| Average Order Value (AOV) | Revenue / Orders | Average revenue per order |
| Order Fulfillment Rate | Orders Fulfilled / Orders Received | Percentage of completed orders |
| Return Rate | Returned Orders / Total Orders | Product return percentage |

# 4. Required Business Dimensions

Metrics become meaningful when they are analyzed across different business dimensions.

MetricMind should support the following common dimensions:

| Dimension | Purpose |
|------------|----------|
| Time | Day, Week, Month, Quarter, Year |
| Geography | Region, Country, City |
| Product | Product, Category, Brand |
| Customer | Customer Segment, Customer Type |
| Sales Channel | Online, Offline, Marketplace |
| Department | Sales, Finance, Marketing |

These dimensions allow users to ask questions such as:

- Revenue by Region
- Profit Margin by Product Category
- Customer Churn by Country
- Monthly Sales Trend
- Quarterly Revenue Growth

# 5. Semantic Metric Definition Template

To maintain consistency, every metric in the Semantic Layer should follow a standard structure.

```yaml
metric_name: gross_margin
description: "Gross profit as a percentage of net revenue"
formula: (net_revenue - cogs) / net_revenue
type: ratio
base_metrics:
  - net_revenue
  - cogs
dimensions:
  - time
  - geography
  - product_category
  - customer_segment
owner: finance
source_tables:
  - orders
  - cost_of_goods
```

Using a common template improves maintainability, governance, and collaboration across the development team.

# 8. Benefits of Using Semantic Metrics

Implementing semantic metrics provides several advantages:

- Establishes a single source of truth for business metrics.
- Ensures consistent calculations across dashboards and reports.
- Prevents AI-generated SQL inconsistencies.
- Improves trust in Business Intelligence systems.
- Simplifies report maintenance.
- Enables governed AI-driven analytics.
- Makes metric definitions reusable across multiple applications.
- Supports transparent and auditable business reporting.
